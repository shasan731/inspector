# Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-07-16

Used Phone Inspection Assistant is designed to work without an account or backend. Inspection records, manually entered IMEI or serial values, seller/customer information, notes, recordings retained as evidence, photos, settings, and generated reports are stored locally in the application's private storage or a destination the user explicitly chooses.

The application does not include analytics, advertising, tracking, remote configuration, or an SDK that uploads crash reports. It does not sell personal information and does not upload inspection information to the developer.

Permissions are requested contextually for camera, foreground microphone recording, vibration, Bluetooth, legacy scan location, or permitted phone-state diagnostics. The application does not read IMEI, IMSI, ICCID, phone number, contacts, SMS, call logs, Wi-Fi MAC address, or other restricted hardware identifiers. A manually entered IMEI is not checked against a blacklist, manufacturer, or ownership database.

Google Play processes product, payment, and purchase-entitlement information when a user purchases or restores the non-consumable `lifetime_pro` upgrade. Google Play's own privacy policy governs that processing. The application caches only whether Pro entitlement is active and does not log or export purchase tokens.

The user controls disclosure. A PDF, JSON backup, image import, save, print, or share occurs only after an explicit action. The Android system picker, Sharesheet, or print service may pass selected content to a destination the user chooses, whose privacy practices then apply.

Users can delete individual inspections, temporary files, or all local inspection data from Settings. Uninstalling normally removes private app data, subject to device/vendor backup behavior; sensitive inspection data and evidence are excluded from this app's cloud backup configuration.

No inspection can certify ownership, IMEI validity, repair history, future performance, or the absence of hidden damage. Android and vendor limitations may make information unavailable.

For a security or privacy concern, follow the private reporting process in `SECURITY.md` rather than posting sensitive inspection content publicly.
